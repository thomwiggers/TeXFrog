\textbf{Claim.}
  Games \tfgamename{kemdem}{G3} and \tfgamename{kemdem}{G4} are identically
  distributed.

With $\mathit{pad}$ uniform and independent of everything else, $c_2 \gets
\mathit{pad} \oplus m_b$ is itself uniform and independent of the challenge bit
$b$. We may therefore sample $c_2$ directly. The adversary's view is now
independent of $b$, so its advantage is zero. Only the final DEM step changes,
so all three interior segments collapse to stubs.
