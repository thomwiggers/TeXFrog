\textbf{Claim.}
  Games \tfgamename{kemdem}{G0} and \tfgamename{kemdem}{G1} are computationally
  indistinguishable assuming the KEM is $\INDCPA$-secure.

We replace the encapsulated key $K$ with a uniformly random key drawn from
$\mathcal{K}$, independent of the KEM ciphertext $c_1$. A distinguisher between
the two games yields a KEM $\INDCPA$ adversary of the same advantage. Only the
\emph{Encapsulation} segment changes, so the cropped render collapses the
\emph{Setup} and \emph{Key derivation} segments.
